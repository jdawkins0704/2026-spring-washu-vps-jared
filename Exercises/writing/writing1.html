What is a ritual, practice, or routine in your life that is important for your psychological wellbeing and/or fulfillment? Why? How does this ritual or behavior contribute to your life? What aspect of it makes it valuable to you? What can it teach us about the nature or ingredients of human fulfillment more broadly?

A ritual/routine that is important for me in my life for my psychological well-being/fulfillment would be running. I love running in all of its forms, whether it's running mileage or sprinting through a finish line. Regardless of the way it’s done or the intention behind it, running has been a huge part of my life that I can’t imagine living without. I initially began running cross country when I was in 3rd grade and began running track & field in 6th grade. Because of this, it is hard for me to remember a time in my life when running wasn’t prevalent. Even now, I am on the track and field team here at WashU, where I get to continue this practice that leaves me feeling so fulfilled. For me, I think that running satisfies a few different things in my mind. For recreational running, where I’m getting mileage in, running is a way for me to delve deep into thought and work through things introspectively, as it’s generally just my body and my mind making it happen. Sprinting kind of does the direct opposite for me. Sprinting is fast-paced and explosive, which is perfect for me when I don’t want to think. It allows me to push my body to its limits and see what the outcome will be. Overall, both of these forms of running contribute positively to my life because they give me ways to either clear my head or ruminate on a thought. I’ve tried other physical outlets, such as basketball or swimming, but neither one of those has allowed me to receive the same mental benefit as running. I have also tried non-physical outlets such as reading (which I also love) and journaling, but there is still something missing that running allows me to satisfy. Another reason why running has become such a routine for me is the way it connects me with others. I usually run alone, but when I run with others, aka my friends, it’s a bonding experience unlike any other. I have been running with the same friends since 3rd grade, both on and off the track. It has been really nice getting a chance to build deeper relationships that way. In terms of what it can teach us about the nature or ingredients of human fulfillment more broadly, I think that running as consistently as I have has shown me that in order for me to feel fulfilled with my life, I need some form of physical activity that keeps my mind and body sharp and in motion. At the end of my freshman year of track and field here at WashU, I broke my femur and spent roughly 9 months unable to run. These 9 months were not only rough because of the physical therapy and rehab for my leg, but also because of the fact that I couldn’t run as a way to clear my head. I essentially just felt stuck spiraling with no outlet to free myself. This also makes me wonder what I am going to do post-grad when I am no longer running track and field as consistently. My goal at that point in my life will be to train for marathons so I can still run, while also having an end goal in sight.
