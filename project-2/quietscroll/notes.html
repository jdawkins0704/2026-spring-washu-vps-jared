Notes on looking

This website interprets Look at Me Like You Love Me by Jess T. Dugan, a collection of photographs that explores intimacy, vulnerability, and the quiet emotional space between people.

Intent

The site uses a slow scrolling layout and minimal design so that each photograph has space to breathe. The goal is to encourage viewers to pause and look closely rather than move quickly through the images.

Structure

The homepage introduces the tone of the work, while the interior pages present smaller image sequences like sections of a photobook. This notes page explains how the website translates the atmosphere of the book into a digital format.

This project is not meant to replicate the book exactly, but to recreate its quiet atmosphere and the feeling of being seen.
