Overall, the documentary Eno really opened my eyes up to what a documentary can be. Initially, going into the documentary, I expected it to be somewhat of a more traditional and linear overview of Brian Eno’s life. I was delightfully surprised when that wasn’t the case, and how generative structure became a main point of the film. It was really cool to realize that no two people walk away with the exact same experience, which helped me recognize the fact that storytelling is extremely subjective. I also liked how the documentary really emphasized Eno’s ideas around creativity and chance. All of that being said, I really enjoyed this documentary as it challenged my assumptions regarding authorship and control, and helped me to think more deeply about how meaning can emerge without everything being tightly structured.
