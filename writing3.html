When do you cherish the slow or hard way of doing something? Why? When do you embrace constraints or prefer inefficiency? When do you willingly sacrifice convenience or comfort? What does the limited, slower, or more effortful approach offer that the quicker and easier way does not? What can we learn from this about human fulfillment and the kinds of technologies that might support it?

I cherish the slow or hard way of doing something when it comes to running and jumping for track and field. To me, this kind of feels like a cop out answer because when it comes to running and jumping, there is no way to skip the slow or hard way. There’s no cheat code or shortcut; you essentially just have to thug it out and dedicate immeasurable amounts of time in hopes of improving. Even when dealing with injury. There is no one-size-fits-all or simple answer as to how to get back on the track, so every step of the process is slow and fairly difficult. I embrace constraints with this by allowing me to slow down and really hone in on my technique. An example of this is jumping. My full approach jump is roughly 18 steps, but at meets earlier in the season, we start from a 12 or 14-step jump. These constraints help me fully embrace triple jump as it forces me to hone in on my phases to ensure that I can jump further. This limited, slower, more effortful approach offers me the chance to look more closely at where I can improve my technique and form without the fear of bringing too much speed or power into the jump. It also enables me to dissect where my strengths are and identify ways to fix my weaknesses. With that, I also think that every time I train or condition myself, I am constantly putting myself in a position where I sacrifice convenience and comfort. In a sport like track and field, every time you run/jump/throw/etc., you are essentially “max repping” whatever you're working on. This means that when it comes to training, there isn’t much room for comfort as you constantly have to push your body to its fullest extent. While this can sometimes feel like a lot, I love it because it enables me to constantly keep pushing the boundaries of what I believe is possible for myself. All of that being said, I feel like we can learn that human fulfillment comes from embracing the uncomfortable and pushing ourselves outside of our comfort zone. Without living in the uncomfortable, we become complacent with mediocrity and limit our potential and growth. I feel as though, in regard to track specifically, there are a lot of technologies that support it. For one, a big thing for me is listening to music as I go on longer runs. It allows me to clear my head and zone out for a little bit. I also use my watch during workouts to keep track of my pacing as well as to keep a list of the different items that may go on our warmup. Technology has been a great asset overall in helping me develop and grow as an athlete.
