What is something you appreciate or long for from the past? Why? It could be something you lived through, or something from the distant past. It might be a tradition or an antiquated value that you appreciate, an old way of doing things that you prefer / are fond of, or even a personal time or cultural moment that you hope to reconnect with. What do you appreciate about it? What wisdom does it contain? What can it teach us about living well today?

One thing that I appreciate, or long for from the past, is the simplicity of living on campus near a lot of my friends. I moved off campus this semester, and while the experience has been a lot of fun, it feels weird being so much further away from a lot of the friends and people that I’m really close with. When we all lived on campus, on the South 40, it was really easy to hang out because everyone’s dorms were right next to each other. The furthest we had to walk was a few minutes. That is not the case now with us all living miles apart, requiring us to drive if we want to hang out. I appreciated the simplicity of just being able to walk over to a friend's place and hang out or study for hours. It was also nice having everything conveniently located on the 40, such as food, a gym, study rooms, etc. Looking back, in terms of wisdom, this shows me that in the future, I would love to live in a place where so many of my friends are nearby. Obviously, there will never be a situation again where we’re all living within seconds or minutes of each other, but finding a healthy balance is something that will be really important to me. In regard to living today, it has also shown me that we often forget about the simple things. People become so fixated on living in the nicest/biggest house or having a large kitchen that they lose out on what is really important, which is being close to the people that they care about. Looking back and appreciating this experience has reminded me that I, too, need to remember and prioritize the little things. Cause those are what matter most at the end of the day.
